# Chapter 5: The Environment is Everything

Let me tell you something that took me way too long to learn: **you don't rise to the level of your goals. You fall to the level of your systems.**

And your environment is the foundation of those systems. Every day, your environment is whispering suggestions to your brain. Make those suggestions work for you, not against you.

## Make Good Habits Easy, Bad Habits Hard

This is the simplest principle in the world. But people get it backwards.

They try to motivate themselves to resist donuts. To force themselves to ignore their phone. To white-knuckle through willpower.

That's fighting uphill.

Instead, design your environment so the good habits are RIGHT THERE and the bad habits require effort.

Want to eat healthy? Keep fruit at eye level in your fridge. Hide the junk food in a hard-to-reach cabinet. Actually, maybe just don't buy it.

Want to out your workout exercise? Lay clothes the night before. Put your running shoes by the door. Keep a yoga mat visible.

Want to stop checking your phone? Put it in another room. Use an app blocker during work hours.

> **REAL TALK:** I used to keep my phone on my nightstand. Every morning, I'd reach for it before I'd reach for anything else. Now it's charging in the kitchen. That one change reduced my morning screen time by 40 minutes. I didn't use more willpower. I just made it harder to fail.

## Friction Accounting

Think of friction like a tax on behavior. Good friction makes bad habits expensive (hard to do). Good habits get a friction discount (easy to do).

Before you start your habit journey, do a "friction audit":

**For your desired habit (make it easier):**
- What can I do to reduce steps between me and the habit?
- What's currently in the way?
- How can I make this take less time/energy to start?

**For the bad habit (make it harder):**
- What's making this habit easy right now?
- How can I add steps between me and the bad behavior?
- What can I remove to make it less accessible?

## Phone Placement Strategies

Phones are the biggest habit killers for most people. Here's how to fight back:

1. **Charge it in another room.** Your morning self will thank you.
2. **Turn off non-human notifications.** Keep only calls and texts from actual humans.
3. **Use grayscale mode.** It makes your phone 50% less addictive. True story.
4. **Keep it in a drawer during work.** Even just knowing it's there is distracting.
5. **No phones in the bedroom.** Get an actual alarm clock if you need one.

You don't need more willpower to beat your phone. You need a different location.

## Workspace Design for Focus

If you work from home like me, your workspace matters more than you'd think.

Quick fixes that help:
- **Clear your desk.** Everything except what you're working on RIGHT NOW.
- **Use a separate browser profile for work.** Don't mix personal and professional.
- **Keep headphones on.** Even if nothing's playing. It's a signal.
- **Have a "shutdown ritual."** Close your laptop at the same time each day.
- **Block distracting sites.** There are free tools for this.

The goal isn't to create the perfect environment. The goal is to remove obvious obstacles. You don't need a minimalist aesthetic. You need to stop making it easy to fail.
